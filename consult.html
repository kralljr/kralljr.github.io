From 2010-2011, I worked as a biostatistician at the Johns Hopkins School of Nursing. There, I worked with faculty, students, and post-doctoral fellows to analyze their data, including SAS and STATA support. During this time, I began working with Jason Farley and Matt Hayat on the risk of MRSA infection in different subpopulations (see published manuscript).

I have also collaborated with students at the Johns Hopkins School of Public Health on PhD theses and Masters in Public Health (MPH) capstone projects. During the 2012 academic year, I served as a teaching assistant for the MPH program where I worked with students completing their capstone research projects.
