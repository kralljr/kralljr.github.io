Welcome! I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Biostatistics and Bioinformatics at Emory University working with Lance Waller. I am primarily interested in developing statistical methods for estimating the associations between particulate matter air pollution and health. I completed my PhD in Biostatistics at Johns Hopkins University, where my advisor was Roger Peng. My research was previously supported by the Epidemiology and Biostatistics of Aging training program where I worked with Michelle Carlson and Qian-Li Xue. Please visit my research page or my Google scholar page for more information.
